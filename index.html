About

I’m Anjana Dayal de Prewitt—humanitarian leader, facilitator, and writer with 25+ years across 30+ countries. My journey began as local humanitarian staff in India, working close to communities and the realities of implementation. Over time, my work expanded across regions and roles, eventually bringing me to Washington, DC—where I have spent the past 15 years engaging with global policy, institutional leadership, and systems change.

This dual experience shapes how I work. I understand the logic of high-level frameworks, and I also understand the questions practitioners ask on the ground: Will this work here? What does this require of people? Who bears the cost of implementation? I help teams design guidance that is ethically grounded, operationally realistic, and human-centered.

Alongside my humanitarian work, I am a published author and poet. Writing is central to how I make meaning—and it informs how I facilitate, advise, and speak.
